Frequently Asked Questions

How does StepGuard track my steps?

▼

StepGuard uses your device's built-in accelerometer and gyroscope sensors to count steps. It can also integrate with popular fitness trackers like Fitbit, Apple Watch, and Google Fit for more accurate tracking.

What happens if I don't meet my daily step goal?

▼

If you don't meet your daily step goal, certain device functions may be restricted based on your settings. This could include limiting app usage time, blocking entertainment apps, or reducing screen brightness. The restrictions are designed to encourage physical activity.

Can I customize my step goals and restrictions?

▼

Yes! StepGuard allows you to set personalized daily step goals and customize which restrictions apply when goals aren't met. You can also set different goals for different days of the week and adjust the severity of restrictions.

What if I have a medical condition that limits my mobility?

▼

StepGuard includes accessibility features and can be configured for users with varying mobility levels. You can set alternative activity goals, adjust step targets, or use manual override features. Please consult with your healthcare provider about appropriate activity levels.

Is my step count data private and secure?

▼

Absolutely. Your step count data is encrypted and stored securely. Most processing happens locally on your device, and we never share your personal health data with third parties without your explicit consent. See our Privacy Policy for complete details.

Can I temporarily disable restrictions for emergencies?

▼

Yes, StepGuard includes an emergency override feature that allows you to temporarily disable all restrictions. This ensures you can always access your device for important calls, messages, or emergency situations.

Does StepGuard work with all fitness trackers?

▼

StepGuard integrates with most popular fitness trackers including Apple Watch, Fitbit, Samsung Galaxy Watch, and Google Fit. It also works with your smartphone's built-in sensors if you don't have a separate fitness tracker.

How accurate is the step counting?

▼

Step counting accuracy depends on your device's sensors and how you carry your phone. Modern smartphones are generally accurate within 5-10% for step counting. For better accuracy, we recommend using a dedicated fitness tracker or wearing your phone consistently.

Can I share my progress with friends or family?

▼

Yes! StepGuard includes optional social features that let you share your progress, compete with friends, or join family challenges. All sharing is opt-in and you control what information you share.

How do I get support if I have issues?

▼

You can reach our support team through the Contact page on our website, by emailing support@stepguard.com, or through the in-app help center. We typically respond to support requests within 24 hours.
